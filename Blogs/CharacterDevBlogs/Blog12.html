Jake Sky Character Rationale

By Michael Parkin | April 15th 2021 | Character Development

When thinking of a fictional character to create, I struggled to make a decision as I knew the type of character that I needed to create would ultimately affect my website design overall. However, after multiple different variations I finally landed on the fictional character of Jake Sky – a creative freelance photographer and videographer. I chose to have a creative character because while I was researching different website designs, I found myself gravitating more towards creative blogs. This was because their visual design and user experience stood out among the rest. As a result, I knew a creative character would give me the creative freedom to create a website similar to these.

However, this is not the only reason I chose to have a creative character. Like Jake, I also enjoy photography – albeit not so much as to make it my career. So, by creating a photography character I would then be able use my own photos to help expand the characters creative portfolio on the website, ultimately fleshing out their character and making them feel more realistic. On top of this, I also have insight into the photography world, which will help when creating character blog posts down the line that feel more realistic.

Although, people may be confused as to why Jake is an American visiting Johannesburg, and not just a South African – the reason for this is twofold. Firstly, this gives Jake an outsider view into South Africa. This allows me to explore Johannesburg from a tourist’s perspective, which I believe will create more interesting observations and creative blog posts. Secondly, it allows me to establish Jake as a ‘researcher’ in South Africa, actively going out to try and find both information about, and photography/videography spots around, the city. This goes hand in hand with what Jake is trying to achieve while in South Africa – film a movie.

Lastly, it is important to understand why I felt that Jakes character would even need a website. As he is a freelancer, it is vital that he has a space to showcase his creative portfolio. While many creatives only have social media to do this, a website can act as a central location to store the best of the freelancer’s work, while also acting as a space for people to easily contact them for work. As such, this creative portfolio will be the focus point of the website as a whole.
