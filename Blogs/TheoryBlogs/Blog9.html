The Medium is the Message and the Web

By Michael Parkin | April 2nd 2021 | Theory

In this blog post I will be looking at a chapter of Marshal McLuhan’s book Understanding Media (McLuhan, 2001), The Medium is the Message, and relating it to the internet. As this writing is quite complex to read, and difficult to interpret, I will start by giving a brief overview of the text before looking at how McLuhan’s text relates, and impacts, the web.

First, it is important to look what he meant by the paradoxical saying The Medium is the Message. In this work, McLuhan explains that people focus too much on the content, the message, that they are receiving and not enough on the medium which the content is presented on. McLuhan even goes as far as to say that “the content of any medium blinds us to the character of the medium”, which reinforces the idea that the distinguishing features that a medium has hold more value than the content because it shapes the way in which the content is created and interpreted – the medium controls the message, and as such it then becomes the message. On top of this, McLuhan also states that the medium allows for new affordances and is an extension of oneself, and as such it ends up shaping the individual.

First, the user navigates the browser and gives it a website domain name to look up. With this, the browser does a Domain Name System (DNS) Lookup in order to find the IP address of the provided domain name. Once found, the browser establishes a connection with the data server through a Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) handshake. If the website is more secure, the browser will also have to perform an extra step of security and establish a Transport Layer Security (TLS) handshake using multiple encryption keys too.

Next, while McLuhan published this work in 1962, before the existence of the internet, he already had an important grasp over new media how they impacted communication. When we look at how his theories relate to the internet, we can start by looking at the way we share content on the internet. The internet encompasses many forms of communication, each having their own different affordances and ways of communicating, one of which are social medias. However, not only do social medias have their own limitations and affordances, but each specific social media has their own unique affordances and ways of communicating. If a user posts something on TikTok their audience assumes it will be short and concise as this is the way the social media has chosen to limit their users. However, if content is posted on YouTube, a user knows to expect longer content. Each social media – medium – thus shapes the content we share on it. This ultimately falls in line with McLuhan’s theory that the medium shapes the content that is created and as such it shapes what individuals expect from different mediums.

Furthermore, this results in a direct impact on the way we develop for the web. With the Web 1.0, the internet was very static and while it was a medium that allowed users to view information, it tended to be more official. With the introduction of the Web 2.0, users were then able to post their own content and as such, new mediums on the internet were created to accommodate this. Developers now have to keep in mind the impact that their website will have and how it will shape the way people interactive with one another, and their site.

In conclusion, although Marshal McLuhan wrote this text before the invention of the internet, he had a firm understanding of the way in which mass communication and different mediums affected the content they were portraying. This relates directly to the internet, ultimately playing a role in how websites should be developed.

References:

  1. McLuhan, M., Fiore, Q., & Fairey, S. (2001b). Medium is the Massage (1st ed.). Gingko Press.
