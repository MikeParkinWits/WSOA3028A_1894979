The Impact of the Dot Com Bubble on the Web

By Michael Parkin | March 19th 2021 | Words: 520 | Theory

The burst of the dot-com bubble resulted in the loss of trillions of dollars, but the affect and impact it had on the internet was huge, laying the groundwork for the future. In this blog post I discuss this by breaking down the internet and World Wide Web before, during, and after the dot-com bubble burst.

Although the World Wide Web was established by Tim Berners-Lee between the late 1980’s and early 1990’s, it was still not easily accessible to many people as it required both expensive hardware and a vast technical knowledge. However, this all changed when the more user focused browsers, Mosaic and Netscape Navigator, launched in 1993 and 1994 respectively. Along with their more user-friendly design, they allowed the internet to be more accessible to the average person as they were available on more platforms. This led to an exponential increase in both internet users and overall web traffic.

This created a shift in entrepreneurs who now saw potential on the internet and creating dot-com companies (companies created around website ideas). This exponential growth of the internet blindsided investors and they quickly invested more money into these companies – being told more money invested now would result in higher profits later. This resulted in the “stock equity valuations” of technology and the internet to grow and led to many dot-com companies IPOs (Initial Public Offerings) gaining exponential growth and unheard-of valuations.

However, the majority of these companies were reporting a loss, focusing their efforts and investors’ money on growth – as investors only caried about this, along with the websites userbase and valuations. But in the early 2000’s things quickly changed when the Japanese economy went into a recession. This acted as a catalyst for the dot-com bubble bursting as investors quickly became more cautious with their money and the dot-com company’s high valuations started to fall. While bigger investors were not badly affected, it was the average investor that was impacted the most – losing trillions of dollars.

Nonetheless, the bursting of the dot-com bubble had its positives. First, this proved as a valuable lesson for both future companies and investors to take profit into account and not only valuation and growth. Secondly, this quick expansion of the internet meant that the infrastructure for the internet needed to expand. This resulted in an important infrastructure of fiber optics being laid down. On top of this, with most of the dot-com companies becoming bankrupt, a large amount of bandwidth on this infrastructure became unused. This paved the way for future companies to come in and expand both easily and cheaply.

So although the bursting of the dot-com bubble resulted in trillions of dollars being lost, it was an important milestone in the development of the internet as it taught both tech companies to seek more than just valuation and growth, and it helped lay the foundation for the future of the internet. Although, as much as it may have taught a lesson, many now argue that we are currently facing a repeat of what happened during the dot-com bubble in the form of the app bubble and a crypto bubble.
