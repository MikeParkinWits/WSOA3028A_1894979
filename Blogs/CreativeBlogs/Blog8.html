Let Me Explain some things...

By Jake Sky | March 29th 2021 | Words: 359 | Creative

Welcome to my first official blog post about, I’m glad you’re still here! But now, you’ve waited long enough – what’s been going on? Like I said in my last post, I’ve been trying to make a movie! But you’re probably thinking to yourself, “Jake, you’re only trying? It’s been so long!”. I know, I know – but hear me out. I think everyone can agree that the last year has been more than crazy, but I’ve been pushing through and I think progress is actually starting to be made with my movie. That brings me to the topic of today, what exactly happened to me that stopped everything in its tracks? Well, let me start at the beginning.

My close friends Casey and Luke approached me mid-way through 2019, ecstatic about this new movie idea that they had started writing. Now not many of you know this, but this wouldn’t be my first run-in with directing a movie – with the first few directing attempts going less than ideally… So, when Casey and Luke approached me and told me that they wanted me on board to direct, I knew this was my chance for redemption. However, Casey and Luke have big dreams, and their crazy idea was to have it set in Zambia - Africa! But it could have been in Antarctica for all I cared, because I was in it for one reason only – to prove myself.

We start planning straight away, and everything was going as smoothly as possible and within months we’re ready to start filming. With our flights booked, all of us are feeling ecstatic about the project. We hope on the plane, and slowly our dreams are becoming a reality - but soon that excitement wore off. Upon landing at our layover in Johannesburg, South Africa, we were greeted with devastating news – the country had gone into a national lockdown because of the Coronavirus pandemic, and we were now stuck.

But that’s where I’ll leave it for today. I know it’s a bit of a cliff hanger, but we do want you coming back for the next one!

Until next time - Stay Motivated, Stay Inspired, and Never Stop Creating.
